Ultra-processed foods impact your memory

A new study discovered that people who eat higher amounts of ultra-processed foods may have a higher risk of cognitive impairment including memory loss, thinking problems, and risk of stroke.7

Here‚Äôs the breakdown from the study:

  • Consuming more ultra-processed foods was associated with a 16% higher risk of cognitive impairment. While consuming more unprocessed or minimally processed foods was linked with a 12% lower risk of cognitive impairment.
  • Additionally, a greater intake of ultra-processed foods was also linked to an 8% increase in risk of stroke, while a greater intake of unprocessed or minimally processed foods was linked to a 9% decreased risk of stroke.

It should be noted that the study does not prove that eating ultra-processed foods causes these brain conditions, but it does show an association.
